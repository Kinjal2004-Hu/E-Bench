CASE: Cyber Fraud via Fake E-Commerce Website

COMPLAINANT: Mr. Rajesh Kumar Sharma, Age 34, Resident of Sector 45, Gurugram, Haryana

ACCUSED: Unknown individuals operating under the name "BestDealMart.in"

FACTS OF THE CASE:

On 15th January 2026, the complainant Mr. Rajesh Kumar Sharma visited a website named "BestDealMart.in" which appeared to be a legitimate e-commerce platform offering electronic goods at heavily discounted prices. The website displayed fake customer reviews, fabricated trust badges, and a fraudulent GST registration number (GSTIN: 06AABCU9603R1ZM).

The complainant placed an order for a Samsung Galaxy S25 Ultra smartphone listed at Rs. 45,000 (MRP Rs. 1,29,999) and a MacBook Air M3 listed at Rs. 52,000 (MRP Rs. 1,14,900). The total transaction amount was Rs. 97,000.

Payment was made via UPI transfer to the account "bestdealmart@ybl" linked to a Punjab National Bank account. After payment, the complainant received an automated email with a fake tracking number (AWB: BD2026011534782).

When the products did not arrive within the promised delivery window of 5-7 business days, the complainant attempted to contact customer support. The phone number listed on the website (9876543210) was switched off. The email support@bestdealmart.in bounced back as undeliverable. By 28th January 2026, the website was taken offline.

Upon further investigation by the complainant:
1. The domain "BestDealMart.in" was registered on 1st December 2025 using fabricated identity documents
2. The bank account receiving payment was opened using forged Aadhaar and PAN documents
3. At least 47 other victims have filed similar complaints against the same entity on consumer forums
4. The total estimated fraud amount across all known victims exceeds Rs. 35,00,000

The complainant filed an FIR at Cyber Crime Police Station, Gurugram on 30th January 2026.

EVIDENCE AVAILABLE:
- Screenshots of the website (archived via Wayback Machine)
- UPI transaction receipts and bank statements
- Email correspondence with fake tracking details
- WhatsApp chat screenshots with the "customer care" number
- Consumer forum complaints from other victims
- Domain registration records from NIXI

RELIEF SOUGHT:
The complainant seeks recovery of Rs. 97,000 along with compensation for mental agony and harassment. Criminal prosecution of the accused persons is also requested.
